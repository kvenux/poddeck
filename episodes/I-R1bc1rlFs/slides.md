# How to Train an Agent in 30 Days

<div class="text-sm mt-2">

The biggest mistake of 2024: vendors said "just turn the product on" and it would magically work. It doesn't.

</div>

<div class="mt-3 text-sm">

Jason describes the actual process:
1. **Upload** your website, wiki, training docs, best emails
2. **Answer questions** the agent asks (it's just Q&A, not scary)
3. **Correct mistakes** every day for 30 days, 1-2 hours each
4. By day 30, it's "pretty good"

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500 rounded text-sm">

**Cheat code**: After training 3 agents, they distilled everything into **one prompt**. Gave that prompt to Agent Force — it was "pretty good" in a day.

</div>

# Train on Your Best Person, Not a Generic Template

<div class="mt-4 text-sm">

The single most important thing Jason learned about making AI emails good:

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-6 text-lg italic border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-20 rounded">

"Take your best person on your sales team, the best marketer you have, take their email copy and use that as a template for your AI."

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

### What Most People Do Wrong
- Use a generic untrained agent
- Give it to junior SDRs and expect magic
- Never upload any real training data
- A $10B+ public company called SaaStr for help — none of their 20 people on the call had done it themselves

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### What Actually Works
- Upload your best person's best emails
- Let the agent create A/B variants from that baseline
- Connect it to data sources (Salesforce, website visitors)
- It personalizes based on real data
- The AI produces a "facsimile of your best person"

</div>

</div>

# Does It Matter If People Know It's AI?

<div class="mt-4 text-sm">

After sending hundreds of thousands of emails, SaaStr tested both approaches.

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### What They Tested
- Emails signed as "Digital Amelia" or "Digital Jason"
- Emails sent without any AI disclosure
- Both approaches across high and low volume campaigns

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### What They Found
- **It doesn't matter.** People don't care as long as the email adds value and they get an instant response.
- Founders sometimes reply: "Haha, I can tell this is AI but it's pretty good. Can I do a meeting?"

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-yellow-50 border-l-4 border-yellow-500 rounded text-sm">

**The real insight**: Most human sales emails aren't great either. Jason recalls inheriting Adobe reps whose emails were "the worst emails I've ever read." The bar for AI to beat the midpack is lower than we think.

- Even Clay starts at ~$100K/year

<div class="mt-3 text-sm opacity-70">

# Advice for Sales Leaders: Do It Yourself

<div class="mt-4 text-sm">

Jason's advice for VPs and leaders worried about becoming obsolete:

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500 rounded">

### The Recipe

<div class="text-sm mt-2 space-y-2">

1. **Pick one problem** — support, SDR, inbound qualification, whatever is most painful
2. **Pick a leading vendor** — one that treats you well and will help you
3. **Do it yourself** — train the agent, ingest the data, do the iterations
4. **Spend 50-60 hours** plus vendor qualification time
5. **Get it live into production**

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500 rounded text-sm italic">

"If you can go do this, you're hyper employable."

</div>

<div class="mt-3 text-sm">

The first one will be brutal. The second will be easier. By the third, you'll distill it into one prompt. Then you become the person every startup wants to hire as their chief agentic GTM officer.

</div>

<div class="mt-3 text-sm opacity-70">

Don't wait for an agency. Don't wait for your team. Don't wait for it to be easy. The agencies don't know how to do this yet. You've got to do it yourself.

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500 rounded">

### The Exercise

<div class="text-sm mt-2 space-y-2">

1. Open your browser **in incognito mode**
2. Go to your own product/website with a fresh Gmail address
3. Try **everything**: support, contact sales, sign up, buy something
4. You will **cry** about how bad some of it is
5. Pick the thing that makes you cry the most
6. **Go buy that agent and fix it**

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500 rounded text-sm italic">

"Pick the thing that makes you cry the most over your mold wine and go buy that agent and fix it."

<div class="mt-3 text-sm">

Most people are so lost in strategic planning that they've forgotten what the actual customer experience is like. How bad support is. How long sales takes to respond. Do this once a year — ideally once a quarter.

# What's Changed and What Hasn't

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

### Permanently Changed
- **Support**: 50-80% done by AI, train has left the station
- **Email-based SDR**: will be mostly extinct within 12 months
- **Inbound BDR/qualification**: similarly extinct
- **RevOps tracking**: everything a human does is now tracked instantly in CRM
- **Buyer expectations**: customers expect the agent to work during the pilot *before* they pay

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### Still the Same
- All the individual **plays** work (events, outbound, webinars, podcasts)
- **Enterprise relationships** still matter for big deals
- **Field sales** and door-knocking still work, maybe better than ever
- Showing up **in person** still closes at higher rates
- You still need **leadership** — VPs, CROs, the C-suite
- **High-dollar whiteboard sales** with 50 named accounts

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-yellow-50 border-l-4 border-yellow-500 rounded text-sm">

**The net-net**: We're going to need more sales and go-to-market professionals than ever — because the winners are growing so quickly that even with AI efficiency, they need more humans. But those humans need different skills.

</div>

# Bonus: The Pluribus Insight

<div class="mt-4 text-sm">

Jason connects the Apple TV show Pluribus (about a connected hive mind) to the future of AI GTM:

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500 rounded">

### Right Now vs. The Future

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-3 text-sm">

<div>

**Today**: Get AI agents to be *almost as good as humans*, but working 24/7 at scale. That alone is a resource you don't have today.

</div>

<div>

**Tomorrow (the Hive Mind)**: When all agents can share data across every tool, know everything that happens, talk to each other in real time — then humans are truly at risk in GTM.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm">
